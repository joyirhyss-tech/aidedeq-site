The Practices

What changes when you actually practice

Not definitions. Not theory. What these practices do in your real, messy, beautiful life.

Mindfulness

You stop reacting on autopilot. You notice the gap between what happens and what you do about it. Arguments get shorter. Decisions get clearer.

Forgiveness

You put down the weight you've been carrying for someone who isn't thinking about you. Your chest loosens. Your energy returns.

Attitudinal Healing

You learn to choose peace over the need to feel right in the moment. Fear loosens its grip. You start responding from love instead of defense.

Our Approach

Analog first. Always.

We don't believe you need an app to breathe. The practice has to hold when your phone is dead, when you're mid-argument, when you're lying awake at 3am.

Tech helps you follow through, track patterns, build tools, stay accountable. But the practice lives in your body first. If it doesn't work offline, it doesn't work.

Emergency Breathing

Five to six breaths each minute. Reset your parasympathetic nervous system.

  1. Stop what you're doing. Sit or stand still.
  2. Breathe in slowly through your nose for 4 seconds.
  3. Hold for 2 seconds.
  4. Breathe out slowly through your mouth for 6 seconds.
  5. Repeat 5-6 times. That's one minute.

Your nervous system will reset. Your heart rate will slow. Your thinking will clear. Master this. Then practice with another person this week.

Thinking Clearly

Align your builds with your intentions. Solve a real problem, not just automate more noise.

So many amazing tools are being used to automate and 10x productivity like every other work slop coming out right now. The AIdedEQ way is different.

Start by truly considering yourself, your family, your friends, your neighborhood, and your community. Then intentionally set your sights on one issue you can actually solve.

The IAMAR Framework:

  • Intention: What do you actually want?
  • Attention: Where is your focus right now?
  • Meaning: Why does this matter to you?
  • Attitude: What lens are you looking through?
  • Response: What's your best next action?

Building from "I don't want this" is different than knowing your values, listening to yourself and others, clearly defining the issues, deciding what's in your sphere of influence, and choosing a path forward. That's the solution route.
