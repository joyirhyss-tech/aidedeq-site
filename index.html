Emergency Breathing

Five to six breaths each minute. Reset your parasympathetic nervous system.

  1. Stop what you're doing. Sit or stand still.
  2. Breathe in slowly through your nose for 4 seconds.
  3. Hold for 2 seconds.
  4. Breathe out slowly through your mouth for 6 seconds.
  5. Repeat 5-6 times. That's one minute.

Your nervous system will reset. Your heart rate will slow. Your thinking will clear. Master this. Then practice with another person this week.

From chatting with AI to automating your work

If you already use ChatGPT or Claude to draft emails, you are one step away from having it do the work, not just describe it. Here is the shortest real path from chatting to automation, with a use case you can ship this week.

Try this today: the grant-report auto-draft

Pick a grant report you already wrote last quarter. Open Claude or ChatGPT and build a Project (Claude) or Custom GPT (ChatGPT). Upload the old report, your logic model, and 3-5 funder requirements as reference files. Then write one instruction: "When I paste new program data, draft a funder-ready update in this exact voice and structure." That is it. Next quarter, paste the data, get a 70% draft in 30 seconds. That is the chat-to-automation bridge.

Why this matters (and why it is different from last year)

In 2025-2026, the major AI tools shipped persistent memory, file context, and agentic actions (the AI can now take steps, not just respond). You no longer need to be technical to build a working tool. You need to be clear about the outcome.

Free tools worth starting with (ranked by time-to-value)

  • Claude Projects — upload reference files once, chat against them forever. Best for writing-heavy nonprofits.
  • ChatGPT Custom GPTs — same idea, plus you can share a private GPT with your team.
  • Google NotebookLM — turn a folder of PDFs, meeting notes, and policies into a searchable brain for your staff. Free.
  • Lovable — describe an app in plain English, get a working web app with a database. Good for intake forms, dashboards, lightweight CRMs.
  • Make.com — connect the tools you already use (Gmail, Airtable, Google Drive) and have AI trigger real actions. Free tier is generous.

The 4-step starter loop (do this once, reuse forever)

  1. Pick one repeating task. Something you do every week or month. Intake notes, meeting summaries, grant updates, volunteer onboarding.
  2. Gather 3-5 real examples. Past outputs in your actual voice. This is your training data.
  3. Build a Project / Custom GPT / NotebookLM notebook. Upload the examples + any policies or templates the AI should follow.
  4. Write one instruction sentence that says what you want the AI to do when you come back. Test it on a new input. Refine the instruction, not the examples.

High-value starter use cases for nonprofit teams

  • Grant report first drafts from program data
  • Board-meeting minutes from a Zoom transcript
  • Plain-language translations of policies for families
  • Weekly fundraising email from your CRM export
  • Intake summaries that flag urgency for case managers
  • Volunteer onboarding FAQs answered from your handbook

Thinking Clearly

Align your builds with your intentions. Solve a real problem, not just automate more noise.

So many amazing tools are being used to automate and 10x productivity like every other work slop coming out right now. The AIdedEQ way is different.

Start by truly considering yourself, your family, your friends, your neighborhood, and your community. Then intentionally set your sights on one issue you can actually solve.

The IAMAR Framework:

  • Intention: What do you actually want?
  • Attention: Where is your focus right now?
  • Meaning: Why does this matter to you?
  • Attitude: What lens are you looking through?
  • Response: What's your best next action?

Building from "I don't want this" is different than knowing your values, listening to yourself and others, clearly defining the issues, deciding what's in your sphere of influence, and choosing a path forward. That's the solution route.
